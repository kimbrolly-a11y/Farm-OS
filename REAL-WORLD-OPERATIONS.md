# FarmOS — Real-World Operations Manual (18 verticals, Malaysia)

**What it actually takes to run each vertical for real** — facilities, breeds/species suited to
tropical Malaysia, stocking, daily workflow, labour, economics, regulations, risks, and how each
feeds the circular loops. Researched against current Malaysia-specific sources (DVS, DoF, MPOB,
MARDI, MOTAC, JAKIM, industry). Companion to `PHASE-2-SYSTEMS-SPEC.md` (sensors/AI).

> Prices are indicative (RM) — confirm with DVS/DoF/MARDI extension, MPOB, or local cooperatives.

---

## 0. Cross-cutting patterns (read this first)

**A. One compliance stack.** Almost every vertical routes through: **DVS** (livestock registration,
premises licensing, movement permits, free "disease-free" cert, and **Animal Welfare Act 2015 / Act 772**
licensing for keeping/exhibiting/training — horses + petting zoo); **MyGAP** (MS 2027 husbandry / crops /
aquaculture — DoF runs the aqua version, DoA the crop version); **JAKIM Halal** for meat/milk/processed;
**MPOB licence + MSPO** for palm; **MeSTI/GMP/HACCP** (MOH) for processed food; **MOTAC** + local council
for hospitality.

**B. Off-grid load tiers are consistent.** *Never-shed (life-support):* aquaculture & aquaponics
**aerators + circulation pumps**, hydroponics/NFT pumps, **incubators mid-hatch**, brooder heat for chicks,
rabbit cooling in a heatwave, **cold-chain** (restaurant/processing/milk), and water pumps to occupied
rooms/animals. *First-to-shed:* pool pumps, guest-room AC (with warning), decorative lighting, EV charging,
non-urgent processing. Maps 1:1 onto the existing crisis ladder.

**C. Circular loops interlock.** `manure + POME + food waste → biogas → energy` · `digestate → liquid
fertiliser` · `organic waste → BSF larvae → animal/fish feed` · `BSF frass + compost → soil` · `palm
fronds/EFB/shell/wood → biochar → soil carbon` · `fish/aquaponics water + greywater (constructed wetland)
→ irrigation`. Every vertical is both a waste source and an input sink for another.

**D. Tropical husbandry.** Open-sided, high-roof, E–W buildings for cross-ventilation; **heat stress is
the No. 1 threat everywhere**; **slatted/raised floors** break the parasite cycle (goats, rabbits);
wet-litter + ammonia control (poultry); **GI parasites (*Haemonchus*)** dominate ruminants → resistant
breeds + rotation + FAMACHA.

**E. Small skilled team, not one person.** Realistic estate headcount is a **lean multi-skilled crew
(a dozen-plus at peak)** with heavy AI leverage. Milking, harvest, guest safety, and events are labour spikes.

**F. Value captured on-site.** Best economics = selling into the farm's own **restaurant, lodging &
agritourism** (retail margin) + **premium tropical products** (kelulut honey, udang galah, Musang King,
kampung chicken, goat milk).

**G. Breeds/species chosen for the tropics** — heat/parasite-adapted crossbreds beat temperate high-output genetics.

**H. Agritourism multiplies everything** — the working farm is the attraction; activities/events fill
weekday/off-peak demand.

# LIVESTOCK

## Dairy Cattle
- **Facilities:** open-sided high-roof loose-housing/free-stall barn, E–W oriented; ~8–12 m²/cow shade + feed bunk + milking parlour; grooved non-slip concrete → manure channel; calf hutches, isolation pen, feed/silage store, cattle race. ~1–2 acres built + 10–20 acres forage for 20–30 cows.
- **Equipment:** bucket/pipeline milking machine (~USD 500–1,500; MilkMan fabricates in MY); stainless **bulk cooling tank** to 4 °C in ~2 h (~200 L ≈ USD 2–4k); fans + feed-line sprinklers (tropical heat abatement); chaff cutter, small tractor, washer, lactometer. *Milking vacuum + refrigeration = life-support electrical loads.*
- **Inputs/cow/day:** ~10–13 kg DM (2.5–3% BW): 30–40 kg fresh Napier/Guinea + 4–8 kg concentrate; heavy use of **oil-palm by-products — OPF silage 30–40% of diet, PKC up to ~50% DM**; mineral lick; **water 50–100 L**.
- **Breeds:** **Mafriwal** (Friesian×Sahiwal, heat/tick tolerant, ~2,337 kg/lactation ≈ 9 L/day) is the mainstay; FS and Jersey crosses. **Avoid pure Holstein.**
- **Stocking/scale:** ~1 cow/0.5–1 acre intensively managed forage; a founder+helper runs 15–30 milkers + followers.
- **Workflow:** twice-daily milking (~5–6 am / 4–5 pm) + immediate chilling + wash; cut/deliver forage 1–2×, scrape barn, AI/heat-check, calf feeding. Year-round breeding, ~13-mo calving interval; conserve wet-season forage as silage.
- **Labour:** ~1 skilled stockperson/15–20 cows + 1 helper; skills: machine-milking hygiene, AI/heat detection, calf rearing, ration balancing, mastitis.
- **Economics:** 8–12 L/cow/day; fresh local milk premium (~RM 3–6+/L). **Feed 60–70% of cost** → palm by-products are the profit lever.
- **Regs:** DVS premises licensing; **MyGAP (MS 2027)**; **JAKIM halal**; DOE effluent + milk hygiene.
- **Risks:** **heat stress** (THI >80 midday — fans+sprinklers, night feeding), **mastitis**, **tick-borne** (*Anaplasma/Babesia/Theileria* in Mafriwal), **FMD** (biosecurity+vaccinate), parasites/fluke, milk fever.
- **Circular:** manure → **biogas** (≥~50 cows economic alone; co-digest w/ poultry/food waste) → digestate fertiliser; grow Napier on-farm; OPF/PKC from palm vertical.
- Sources: [OPF/PKC review](https://scialert.net/fulltext/?doi=ajbs.2018.157.164) · [Mafriwal, ILRI](https://www.ilri.org/knowledge/publications/development-mafriwal-dairy-cattle-malaysia) · [MyGAP](https://www.kpkm.gov.my/en/incentive-and-grant/malaysian-good-agricultural-practice-mygap) · [tick-borne disease, PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11296174/) · [heat stress, MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2076-2615/15/2/249)

## Dairy Goats
- **Facilities:** **raised slatted-floor house** (slats 1.2–1.5 m up — faeces drop through, breaks parasite cycle); ~1.5–2 m²/doe + browse paddock; separate kidding/buck (downwind — odour taints milk)/sick pens + clean milk room. Fits on 1–3 acres incl. forage.
- **Equipment:** goat milking machine or hand-milk on a raised stand; small cooling tank/fridge (50–200 L); chaff cutter, hay racks, hoof shears, pasteuriser if bottling.
- **Inputs/doe/day:** ~1.5–2.5 kg DM (3–5% BW): ad-lib forage (Napier silage, **leucaena/legumes**, browse) + 0.4–1.0 kg concentrate; **PKC ≤~80 g/kg for goats** + treated OPF; mineral block; water 4–8 L.
- **Breeds:** **Saanen** (top volume, heat-sensitive), **Anglo-Nubian** (heat-tolerant, high butterfat), **Jamnapari** (hardy); most commercial does are **Saanen × local/Jamnapari** crosses.
- **Stocking:** space/capital-light — one operator runs 30–80 milkers; ~1 buck/25–40 does or AI. Land driven by forage, not floor space.
- **Workflow:** twice-daily milking + chilling; morning concentrate, forage top-ups, kid feeding, muck under slats; year-round breeding (~150-day gestation), ~2 kiddings/3 yr; FAMACHA + hoof trimming.
- **Economics:** peak ~0.8 L/day (Saanen ~833 g/d); **fresh goat milk is premium** direct/agritourism; feed 60–70%; cited ~30–40% ROI; live goats **RM 25–35/kg**.
- **Regs:** DVS + **MyGAP** + **JAKIM halal**.
- **Risks:** **GI parasites (*Haemonchus*)** dominant → slats + rotation + FAMACHA; mastitis; CAE/CCPP/pneumonia; foot rot; heat stress (Saanens); kid coccidiosis.
- **Circular:** dry pellet manure composts direct or → **biogas** (goat+chicken manure documented in MY); browse leucaena/orchard understory; PKC/OPF from palm; whey → poultry/compost.
- Sources: [slatted housing/parasites](https://www.animbiosci.org/journal/view.php?doi=10.5713%2Fajas.19.0272) · [PKC in goats, PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9495131/) · [milk yield, UniSZA](https://journal.unisza.edu.my/agrobiotechnology/index.php/agrobiotechnology/article/download/286/275/) · [biogas goat+chicken](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/318092119_BIOGAS_PRODUCTION_FROM_GOAT_AND_CHICKEN_MANURE_IN_MALAYSIA)

## Sheep (silvopasture under oil palm)
- **Facilities:** extensive grazing under palm; light infra — raised/dirt-floor night kraal (predator/handling/supplement), handling yard + race + footbath, paddock fencing (electric) for rotation, distributed water; ~1–1.5 m²/head covered.
- **Equipment:** electric-fence energiser+reels, water troughs, weigh scale + drafting race, drench guns, hoof shears, ATV/trailer, PKC supplement troughs. **Sheep replace the mower.**
- **Inputs/head/day:** free grazing of palm understory (grasses, ferns, soft weeds) + **PKC/OPF supplement** + mineral lick; water 2–4 L; 30–40 kg ewe needs ~1–1.5 kg DM.
- **Breeds:** **hair/meat breeds** — **Dorper** (Malaysian palm-grazing choice), **Barbados Blackbelly** (parasite-tolerant, aseasonal), Damara, Santa Ines; crossbred with **Malin/Kelantan** locals.
- **Stocking:** ~3–6 mature sheep/ha under mature palm (documented 2–14/ha); move at ~60% forage grazed. A few hundred head feasible with enough open/young-palm blocks.
- **Workflow:** graze dawn→dusk, rotate paddocks, kraal at night (predator/theft); weekly FAMACHA/BCS/lameness; year-round breeding (aseasonal, ~150-day gestation, ~3 lambings/2 yr); batch-mate into drier low-worm windows.
- **Labour:** very efficient — one shepherd ~200–400 head + helper at lambing; skills: rotational grazing, FAMACHA, lambing, predator/theft control.
- **Economics:** **dual value — weed-control savings for palm + meat**; live ~RM 25–35/kg; mutton is import-deficit (strong demand); low input → good margin.
- **Regs:** DVS registration/movement; **MyGAP**; **JAKIM halal** (licensed slaughter).
- **Risks:** **GI nematodes (*Haemonchus*)** override; foot rot; fly-strike; heat (canopy mitigates); predation/theft; forage shortfall under closed canopy; avoid soil compaction near young palms.
- **Circular:** replaces herbicide/mechanical weeding, **manures palms in situ**, converts understory + PKC/OPF to meat; surplus kraal manure → compost/biogas.
- Sources: [oil palm–livestock integration, Frontiers](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/sustainable-food-systems/articles/10.3389/fsufs.2021.640285/full) · [Dorpers graze MY palm](https://www.farmweekly.com.au/story/5749668/dorpers-to-graze-malay-palm-plantation/) · [parasite-resistance ranking](https://www.blackbellysheep.org/articles-useful-links/articles/resistance-to-internal-parasites/)

## Poultry (layers + broilers + hatchery)
- **Facilities:** **open-sided deep-litter houses** E–W, ridge-vent + wide overhangs, mesh sides + roll-down curtains, 8–15 cm litter (rice husk/shavings). Separate **brooder** (32–35 °C wk1, −3 °C/wk), biosecure **incubation room** (~25 °C), layer section (1 nest/4–5 hens). Free-range paddocks/chicken tractors for the eco brand. Foot-dips at every entrance.
- **Equipment:** pan feeders + **nipple drinkers** (cut wet litter); **cabinet incubator + hatcher** + candler; optional solar auto pop-doors, plucker/scalder line. *Brooder heat + incubators = big continuous loads; incubator = never-shed mid-hatch.*
- **Inputs:** feed ~65–70% of cost (starter 22% CP → grower → finisher; layer 16–18% CP + oyster-shell). Broiler ~110–120 g/day (~3.5–4 kg to market); layer ~110–120 g/day; water ~1.6–2× feed; rice-husk bedding.
- **Breeds:** layers **ISA Brown/Lohmann/Hisex** (~300–330 eggs/yr); broilers **Cobb 500 / Ross 308** (~30–35 d to ~2 kg); **Ayam Kampung** (slow, hardy, premium — the eco-restaurant fit) + MARDI **KUB** lines.
- **Stocking:** deep-litter ~3–4 layers/m², broilers ~8–12/m² (lower in open heat). Starters: 200–500 layers, broiler batches 300–500 all-in/all-out, breeding nucleus 30–50.
- **Workflow:** feed/water AM+PM, collect eggs 1–2×/day, walk flock, litter management, log incubator. **Chicken incubation 21 d** (candle ~d7–10, lockdown d18, humidity 65–70%). Broiler ~30–35 d, ~5 cycles/yr; layer point-of-lay ~18–20 wk, lay to ~72–80 wk.
- **Economics:** ~300–330 eggs/hen/yr; broiler **FCR ~1.6–1.7**; Ayam Kampung + free-range eggs premium direct-to-guest.
- **Regs:** **DVS** licensing + free **disease-free cert** (needs biosecurity + records, skips 42-day permit wait); **MyGAP**; **JAKIM halal** slaughter.
- **Risks:** **Newcastle + avian influenza** (vaccinate/biosecurity), coccidiosis, fowl pox, IBD/cholera, **heat stress** (No.1), ammonia from wet litter.
- **Circular:** litter → **BSF larvae** (40–60% protein, 15–18 d cycle) fed back to poultry/fish; **frass** → hydroponics/orchard; manure → compost/biogas.
- Sources: [Poultry in Malaysia](https://www.asiafarming.com/poultry-farming-in-malaysia-a-review-of-malaysian-poultry-industry-with-cost-and-profits) · [FLFAM broiler](https://flfam.org.my/industry-info/the-poultry-industry/broiler-production/) · [DVS disease-free certs](https://codeblue.galencentre.org/2025/10/free-disease-free-health-certs-let-farmers-skip-42-day-livestock-permit-wait-dvs/) · [BSF circular, MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2077-0472/16/3/309)

## Ducks
- **Facilities:** simple shelter + fenced yard + **bathing water** (shallow lined pond/channel, drainable — not stagnant mud); dry predator-proof night house, thick litter (ducks wet it fast → drainage critical); no perches/high nests. Rice-field/orchard "rice-duck" integration.
- **Equipment:** **wide/deep drinkers** (bill-dipping), incubator+hatcher (longer cycle, higher humidity), plucker/scalder (ducks harder to pluck).
- **Inputs:** duck rations + **extra niacin** (leg health); superb foragers (pond weed, snails, greens, spent grain, **BSF**); adult ~150–200 g feed/day; high water need — recirculate to irrigation/aquaponics.
- **Breeds:** **Khaki Campbell** (250–340 eggs/yr, no lighting needed — DVS breeds it), **Indian Runner** (layer/forager), **Muscovy/itik serati** (heat-tolerant lean meat, broody "natural incubator", ~50–100 eggs/yr), **Pekin** (fast meat). Common mix: Khaki (eggs) + Muscovy (meat + brooding).
- **Stocking:** ~3–4 ducks/m² + generous run; flocks 50–200 integrate with ponds/orchard.
- **Workflow:** release at dawn (collect early-morning eggs), refresh bathing water, herd back dusk. **Common duck incubation ~28 d, Muscovy ~35 d** (don't mix in one hatcher); higher humidity + last-week cooling. Pekin meat ~7–8 wk.
- **Economics:** Khaki 250–300+ eggs/yr (fresh/salted/century); Muscovy niche premium meat; classic low-cost high-return smallholder enterprise.
- **Regs:** same DVS/disease-free/MyGAP/JAKIM as chicken; ducks are **silent AI carriers** → separate from chickens + surveillance.
- **Risks:** **avian influenza reservoir**, ND, duck plague/hepatitis, botulism from stagnant water, niacin-deficiency legs.
- **Circular:** duck water → aquaponics/irrigation; rice-duck pest/weed control + manuring; eat BSF/snails/scraps; Muscovy = living incubator (cuts off-grid hatch load).
- Sources: [DVS Breeding Policy (Khaki/Muscovy centre)](https://www.dvs.gov.my/dvs/resources/user_1/DVS%20pdf/Livestock_Breeding_Policy.pdf) · [FAO Duck Keeping in the Tropics](https://edepot.wur.nl/453594) · [Cornell — 28 vs 35-day hatch](https://www.vet.cornell.edu/animal-health-diagnostic-center/programs/duck-research-laboratory/hatching-duck-eggs)

## Rabbits
- **Facilities:** **elevated wire hutches** under open shaded shelter; droppings drop to a pit/tray. **Ventilation + cooling is everything** — rabbits suffer >28–30 °C (misting, frozen bottles, ceramic tiles, shadiest/breeziest spot). Separate buck/doe-with-nest/grower cages; below-cage BSF/vermicompost bed.
- **Equipment:** J-feeders, nipple/crock drinkers, nest boxes, hay racks, tattoo/scale, **cooling gear** (important heat load), simple dispatch/processing station (no plucking).
- **Inputs:** rabbit pellet 16–18% CP high-fibre + **farm greens** (Napier, sweet-potato/kangkung vine, leucaena, banana, veg trim) + hay; grower ~110–170 g/day, lactating doe up to ~0.7–0.9 kg; constant water.
- **Breeds:** **New Zealand White** (meat standard, ~2 kg by 10–12 wk), **Californian**, **NZW×Cali** crosses (~2 kg at ~60 d); heat-adapted Hyla/Hycole.
- **Stocking:** one/cage for growers/breeders; ~10 does + 2 bucks starter; modular hutch banks.
- **Workflow:** feed AM/PM, water (critical in heat), heat-stress watch, clean pit, nest checks. **Gestation ~31 d**, litter 4–8 kits, wean 6–8 wk, up to ~5–6 litters/yr; fryers ~2 kg at 8–12 wk.
- **Economics:** high output/space; MY live ~RM 30/kg + ~RM 8 processing (~RM 38/kg processed) — **niche premium** (Melaka/Kelantan markets). **Market caveat:** limited mainstream demand + cultural/religious sensitivity → position for own restaurant/tourists/specialty.
- **Regs:** DVS registration/permit; MyGAP; JAKIM if halal; confirm local council stance.
- **Risks:** **heat stress** (dominant, fatal, cuts fertility), coccidiosis, snuffles (*Pasteurella*), weaning enteritis (need fibre), mange, sore hocks, RHDV; sudden diet change = fatal gut upset.
- **Circular:** droppings = **"cold" ready-to-use fertiliser** (no composting) direct to orchard/hydroponics; pit → vermicompost/BSF → fish/poultry feed; eats farm forage & trimmings; enriches petting-zoo.
- Sources: [Melaka rabbit industry, The Star](https://www.thestar.com.my/metro/metro-news/2026/04/25/room-for-melaka-rabbit-meat-industry-to-hop-ahead) · [Rabbit Ear Farm Penang](https://rabbitearfarms.com/) · [heat stress, Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00484-021-02191-0) · [legality/acceptance](https://weirdkaya.com/many-are-against-the-sale-of-rabbit-meat-but-is-it-legal-in-malaysia/)

## Horses (stables + guest riding)
- **Facilities:** box stalls ~3.5×3.5 m (ponies 3×3 m), covered aisle, **max natural ventilation** (open ridge, half-doors, wide eaves); all-weather sand/fibre arena (≥20×40 m) + ideally a **covered arena** for storm-proof lessons; grass turnout paddocks (rotate); **dehumidified tack room** (leather molds fast); dry rodent-proof feed/hay store; wash bay + misting fans; farrier + vet space.
- **Equipment:** saddlery, **guest-size helmets + body protectors** (liability-critical), mucking + grooming kits, hoof/farrier tools, hay nets, auto-fill troughs, rugs/fly-sheets, small tractor for hay/muck/arena harrow, fly-control system.
- **Inputs/horse/day:** forage ~1.5–2.5% BW (~8–12 kg hay/grass for 450–500 kg; soak hay to cut dust); hard feed 1–4 kg + salt/electrolytes; water 25–55 L; bedding (shavings/rice husk/rubber mats).
- **Breeds:** hardy heat-tolerant docile types — local ponies, Australian Stock Horse, **re-schooled ex-racehorse TB crosses**, Arabs; ponies (Welsh/Shetland) for kids. Temperament ("bombproof") > breed for guests.
- **Stocking:** small agritourism op 6–15 horses (MY schools 20–35+); ~1–1.5 acres grazing/horse if pasture-fed.
- **Workflow:** AM feed/hay, muck, turnout, health check, groom+tack, lessons/trail rides (**early-morning & late-afternoon to avoid midday heat**), midday hose-down, PM feed. Farrier every 4–8 wk; deworming/vaccines/dental; monsoon = mud/hoof issues.
- **Labour:** ~1 groom/6–10 horses + **qualified instructors** (guest safety) + head groom; visiting vet/farrier. MEF/centres run certification.
- **Economics:** group lessons ~RM 100–160/45 min, private RM 130–300+, hourly ~RM 100–120, pony joyride ~RM 10, trail ride RM 100–150/pax; upkeep **~RM 8,000–18,000/horse/yr** ex-labour.
- **Regs:** **Animal Welfare Act 2015 / Act 772** (DVS) — licensing + Codes of Practice incl. animal-training provider; **public-liability insurance + rider waivers + helmets** essential.
- **Risks:** **colic** (top killer — moldy/wet hay, feed change, dehydration), laminitis/lameness, **heat/dehydration**, hoof disease in monsoon mud, ticks/worms, **guest injury** (falls/kicks/bites — top insurance exposure).
- **Circular:** ~1.5–3 ft³ manure+bedding/horse/day → compost (fertilises orchard/hydroponics) or biogas; graze farm Napier/Guinea + cut hay; spent bedding → recycling vertical.
- Sources: [tropical stable stress, PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11311062/) · [Mad Barn hot-weather + cost](https://madbarn.com/how-much-does-it-cost-to-own-a-horse/) · [Selangor Turf Club rates](https://www.selangorturfclub.com/equestrian/riding-rates/) · [Act 772](https://www.globalanimallaw.org/downloads/database/national/malaysia/Malaysia-Animal-Welfare-Act-2015.pdf)

## Petting Zoo
- **Facilities:** shaded contact pens with **animal retreat/rest areas**; **double barriers** separating animals from food-service zones; **handwashing = the single most important facility** (running-water + sanitiser at every entrance/exit, child height — directly prevents STEC/E.coli outbreaks); species shelter (goat/pony shed, raised shaded rabbit/guinea-pig hutches, duck house+pool); low child-safe rails, non-slip one-way paths ending at handwash; quarantine pen for new/sick animals.
- **Equipment:** hutches/pens, troughs, rodent-proof feed bins, **controlled visitor feed dispensers** (sell approved pellets), grooming + muck tools, handwash/sanitiser stations, signage, shade/misting, first-aid.
- **Animals:** placid domesticated herbivores — **dwarf/pygmy goats, sheep, rabbits, guinea pigs (need vit-C pellets), ducks, chickens, ponies/mini-donkeys**; favour heat-hardy locals (Katjang/Boer goats, kampung chickens, itik). Use tame hand-raised individuals; avoid high-zoonosis contact for young kids.
- **Stocking:** mixed ~20–40 animals; goats ~20 sq ft shelter each; match numbers to visitor volume so animals rest.
- **Workflow:** AM feed/water, muck+disinfect, **isolate any sick/diarrhoeic animal immediately**, restock feed + handwash supplies, supervise contact, rotate/rest midday, secure at night; periodic deworm/vaccinate/hoof.
- **Labour:** keepers + **trained supervisor in every active contact enclosure** (manage child–animal handling + direct handwashing); DVS-registered vet.
- **Economics:** entry ~RM 10–35 + high-margin **feed cups RM 3–5** + pony joyride ~RM 10; value is **footfall/dwell-time driving café/lodging/other-vertical spend** (agritourism diversification, not standalone earner).
- **Regs:** **Act 772 / DVS** exhibition licensing; **public-liability insurance**, zoonosis/handwashing signage, supervised contact (NASPHV/CDC benchmark).
- **Risks:** **zoonotic enteric disease to children** (STEC, Salmonella, Crypto, Campylobacter — animals shed while healthy, so handwashing is non-negotiable), bites/scratches, over-handling stress, heat, night predators, outbreak reputational/insurance hit.
- **Circular:** goat/rabbit/duck/pony manure → compost/fertiliser; ducks → pest control + pond fertilisation; feed farm forage + restaurant/hydroponics trimmings; bedding → recycling.
- Sources: [CFSPH visitor protection](https://www.cfsph.iastate.edu/thelivestockproject/protecting-people-animals-visitors/) · [NASPHV 2023 Compendium](https://www.health.ny.gov/diseases/communicable/zoonoses/docs/2023_nasphv_animal_settings_guidelines.pdf) · [Penn State petting-zoo agritourism](https://extension.psu.edu/agritourism-diversification-petting-zoos-on-the-farm)

# AQUACULTURE

## Aquaculture (fish & prawn ponds)
- **Facilities:** cluster of **earthen grow-out ponds** (0.2–0.5 ha, 1.2–1.5 m deep, clay bottom to a monk/sump), gravity-fed from a header reservoir; **HDPE-lined/fibreglass tanks** for hatchery/nursery + high-density catfish; **sedimentation/treatment pond** on outflow (MyGAP). Separate species; independent inlet/outlet per pond (biosecurity); perimeter bund + predator netting.
- **Equipment:** **paddlewheel/aspirator aerators** — most critical (~1 HP/400–600 kg biomass, run night/pre-dawn); pumps + well backup; **DO/temp/pH meters with low-DO alarm** (never-shed load); seine/cast nets, bar graders, belt feeders (catfish); generator for aerators.
- **Inputs:** GIFT/red tilapia fingerlings, African catfish fry, **udang galah PL** (supply bottleneck; neo-female all-male lines lift yield); floating pellet (tilapia 28–32% CP, catfish 35–42% CP; **FCR tilapia ~1.5–1.8, catfish ~1.0–1.5**); lime + probiotics + pond fertiliser.
- **Species:** **red hybrid / GIFT tilapia** (backbone, >10% growth/generation), **African catfish/keli** (hardy, air-breathing, fastest), **udang galah/*M. rosenbergii*** (premium, big import gap → strong margin).
- **Stocking:** tilapia earthen ~2–5/m² (intensive ~15 kg/m² @150 d); catfish 5–10/m² pond, 20/m³ cage; udang galah grow-out 10–15 PL/m².
- **Workflow:** **feed 2–3×/day to satiation** (cut on hot/cloudy low-DO days); **DO+temp at dawn & dusk daily**, pH/ammonia several×/wk; aerate nightly; 10–30% exchange when ammonia climbs; sample-weigh q2–4 wk. Cycles: tilapia 5–8 mo (400–600 g), catfish 3–4 mo, udang galah 4–6 mo (**cull-harvest** big males). Sun-dry+lime pond between cycles.
- **Economics:** tilapia survival ~70–90%, retail ~RM 18–19/kg (farmgate ~RM 7–10); udang galah **1.2–1.5 t/acre**, ~50% survival, retail **RM 68–120/kg**; startup RM 20–100k; catfish cheapest/fastest.
- **Regs:** **DoF registration (ARF-01)** + biosecurity inspection; **MyGAP aquaculture** (~19 requirements, 2-yr cert, 6-monthly audit); **DoE effluent** (keep wastewater records ≥2 yr).
- **Risks:** **overnight low-DO crashes** (cloud + high biomass + aerator failure = dawn wipeout → aerators = life-support), ammonia/nitrite spikes, **Streptococcus in tilapia** (up to 70% mortality; >27 °C + low DO + high ammonia), TiLV/Aeromonas, prawn white-spot (DO<3, ammonia>0.1), predators, monsoon flooding.
- **Circular:** pond water/effluent → **fertigation/irrigation**; dried sludge → compost; **BSF + duckweed/azolla → partial feed**; solar aerators + battery/genset align with off-grid loop; treatment pond = constructed wetland.
- Sources: [SEAFDEC Aquaculture in Malaysia](https://repository.seafdec.org.ph/bitstream/handle/10862/113/adsea94p125-135.pdf?isAllowed=y&sequence=1) · [DoF MyGAP](https://www.dof.gov.my/en/services/certification-scheme/mygap-certification/) · [udang galah review](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/302923427_Giant_Freshwater_Prawn_Macrobrachium_rosenbergii_Farming_A_Review_on_its_Current_Status_and_Prospective_in_Malaysia) · [Streptococcus tilapia, MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2076-2615/10/11/2141)

## Aquaponics (coupled fish + veg)
- **Facilities:** recirculating loop fish tanks → settler → biofilter → plant beds → sump → pump back; **under rain shelter + insect net + 30–50% shade** (tropics); round fibreglass/HDPE tanks (1–5 m³); **media beds (herbs/fruiting) + DWC rafts (leafy greens)** + mineralisation tank.
- **Equipment:** low-head circulation pump (turn full volume ~hourly), air pump/blower + diffusers, swirl settler + biofilter, **DO/pH/EC/temp meters**, **backup aeration on generator/battery** (pump/air loss kills fish + stalls nitrification in hours).
- **Inputs:** tilapia fingerlings (FCR 1.5–2); **fish feed is the sole major nutrient input**; supplement K/Ca/Fe + pH buffer (system trends acidic); seeds/seedlings; nitrifying bacteria to cycle biofilter.
- **Species:** **tilapia** (standard) / catfish / udang galah polyculture; plants: **lettuce, kangkung (ideal warm-water), pak choi, basil, mint**, then tomato/chilli/cucumber in media beds.
- **Stocking:** design by **feed-rate ratio ~40–60 g feed/m² plant area/day** (MY work: 60 g/day/m² best growth, FCR ~1.67); fish ~10–20 kg/m³ tank with strong aeration.
- **Workflow:** feed 1–3×/day (records = nutrient dosing); daily pump/air + fish + solids check; DO/temp daily, pH/ammonia/nitrite/nitrate/EC q few days; biofilter cycles 2–6 wk; continuous staggered green harvest; fish batches ~5–8 mo.
- **Economics:** **two products from one feed input**; premium greens weekly + fish; higher capex/power than ponds but water/land-efficient + tour-able; best economics = high-value herbs/greens **direct to own restaurant/guests**.
- **Regs:** fish under **DoF/MyGAP**; produce under **MyGAP (crops)**; near-zero discharge = compliance advantage.
- **Risks:** **power/pump/air failure top risk** (life-support), pH drift/acidification, K/Ca/Fe deficiency chlorosis, ammonia/nitrite spikes, tropical heat/DO, tilapia pathogens; **no pesticides/antibiotics** (cross-contaminate loop) → prevention/biocontrol only.
- **Circular:** circular by definition (~90% less water, zero synthetic fertiliser); restaurant waste + **BSF → fish feed**; solids → compost; surplus nutrient water → irrigation; feeds restaurant/lodging + sustainability showcase.
- Sources: [tilapia/lettuce aquaponics review, Wiley](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1155/2024/2642434) · [feeding optimization, Frontiers](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/sustainable-food-systems/articles/10.3389/fsufs.2025.1681782/full) · [Ten Guidelines](https://aquaponics.com/wp-content/uploads/articles/Ten-Guidelines-for-Aquaponics.pdf)

# CROPS & PLANTATION

## Hydroponics (greenhouse leafy + fruiting)
- **Facilities:** tropical **rain-shelter/net-house** (4–5 m gutter, UV-poly roof, 50-mesh roll-up sides, ridge vents — sealed glasshouses unworkable in MY heat); **NFT/DWC** for greens, **substrate fertigation** (cocopeat/perlite polybags + drippers) for fruiting veg. MARDI promotes fertigation (free training).
- **Equipment:** fertigation/dosing controller (A+B injectors, EC/pH auto-correct), delivery + **backup circulation pump** (NFT crop dies in hours on pump failure), misting/fogging, insect net, nursery, EC/pH meters, chilled holding room.
- **Inputs:** two-part **AB Mix** salts (Ca-nitrate kept separate), **cocopeat** (local coir) + perlite, rockwool/cocopeat plugs, rainwater at pH 5.5–6.5; leafy EC ~0.8–1.2, fruiting EC 2.0–3.5.
- **Varieties:** leafy — butterhead/oakleaf/coral/romaine lettuce + Asian greens (pak choy, choy sum, kai lan, bayam) + herbs; fruiting — cherry/beefsteak **tomato, chilli, Japanese cucumber, rockmelon** (flagship). Lettuce best in Cameron Highlands cool zone; lowland leans Asian greens + fruiting.
- **Scale/yields:** ~20–25 plants/m² NFT, lettuce ~2.5 kg/m²/cycle; greens **30–45 d transplant-to-harvest** (6–10 crops/yr); tomato long crop w/ continuous pick; rockmelon ~75–90 d.
- **Workflow:** daily EC/pH correct + pump/dripper + pest scout + stock top-up; **staggered succession sowing** so something always harvesting; transplant seedlings ~2–3 wk. Monsoon = disease + low light (shade/vent).
- **Labour:** low headcount, skilled — nutrient management (EC/pH/dosing), IPM, irrigation maintenance; failures are chemical + mechanical, not field labour.
- **Economics:** hydroponic lettuce/specialty greens well above field prices via supermarket/restaurant; rockmelon = margin star; front-loaded capex, fast repeatable revenue.
- **Regs:** **MyGAP** (DoA) for food-safety + retail/export; MARDI free training.
- **Risks:** **pump/power failure** (crop death in hours — off-grid critical), nutrient lock-out, tip-burn, **downy mildew/Pythium/Fusarium** (humidity), whitefly/thrips/aphids/leafminer, channel algae.
- **Circular:** cocopeat is a local by-product; spent substrate composts to orchard; **aquaponics fish water can supplement mineral nutrient**; rainwater harvest; runoff recirculated.
- Sources: [Sunway XFarms hydroponics MY](https://sunwayxfarms.com/stories/blog/hydroponic-farming-malaysia/) · [MARDI fertigation](https://blogmardi.wordpress.com/2017/02/02/sustainable-food-production-through-fertigation-system/) · [NFT lettuce cultivars, Frontiers](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/plant-science/articles/10.3389/fpls.2025.1639002/full)

## Fruit Orchard (papaya, banana, mango, durian)
- **Facilities:** well-drained land, **raised beds/mounds + drainage ditches** (waterlogging kills papaya/durian); spacing — papaya 2.5–3×1.5–2 m (~790–1,300/acre), banana 2–3 m, mango 6–8 m, durian 8×8 m (~63/acre) or 6×6 (~112/acre); windbreaks, drip/micro-sprinkler grid, packing/ripening shed. Intercrop papaya/banana between young durian for early income.
- **Equipment:** drip fertigation + injector, mist blowers, **fruit-picking poles + catch nets** (mango/durian), pruning saws, brush-cutter, mini-transporter, ripening room, cold store; durian netting/cushioning at ripening.
- **Inputs:** grafted/TC seedlings, split **NPK + micronutrients** (heavy K at fruiting for Brix), compost/manure at planting, **mulch** (banana pseudostem, palm frond/EFB, rice husk), lime, boron/Mg for papaya; irrigate dry spells, drain monsoon.
- **Varieties:** papaya **Eksotika/Eksotika II** + Sekaki; banana **Berangan** (premium), Cavendish, Raja, cooking types; mango **Chok Anan, Harumanis**; durian **Musang King (D197), Black Thorn (D200), D24, Red Prawn** (Musang King flagship). *(MD2 = pineapple, not papaya.)*
- **Scale/yields:** papaya ~30–60 t/ha/yr (bears ~9–11 mo, ~2–3 yr life), banana Cavendish 30–40 t/ha, mango bears ~3–4 yr, durian bears ~5 yr (~20 → 100+ fruit/tree).
- **Workflow:** daily/weekly irrigation + pest scout + weeding + spray + **near-continuous papaya/banana harvest** (year-round); **durian/mango seasonal** (durian after dry spell, mainly Jun–Aug + Nov–Dec, intense 6–10 wk windows); seasonal pruning/fertiliser/thinning.
- **Economics:** papaya farmgate ~RM 1.50–2.50/kg, Berangan banana ~RM 2.25/kg; **durian is the profit engine** — Musang King farmgate swings RM 10–15 (glut) to RM 35–40/kg; papaya/banana steady cash between durian seasons.
- **Regs:** **MyGAP** (DoA) — required for premium retail + durian export to China; phytosanitary for export.
- **Risks:** papaya **dieback/ringspot/anthracnose/Phytophthora**; banana **Fusarium TR4/bunchy-top/Sigatoka**; mango anthracnose/fruit fly; durian **Phytophthora**/stem borer/fruit drop; flood/drought; fruit fly across all.
- **Circular:** palm fronds/EFB → mulch; compost from prunings/pseudostems/waste; **aquaponics effluent + rainwater** fertigate; reject fruit → poultry/petting-zoo/compost; banana leaves → restaurant; **bees pollinate** mango/durian.
- Sources: [Eksotika papaya, Frontiers](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/plant-science/articles/10.3389/fpls.2018.01380/full) · [Musang King spacing](https://www.malaysialand.asia/property-type/agriculture-durian-musang-king-orchard-mixed-durian-farm/) · [durian price swings](https://e.vnexpress.net/news/business/economy/malaysia-durian-price-crash-musang-king-black-thorn-d24-fall-to-as-low-as-2-4-per-kilogram-5001342.html)

## Oil Palm Plantation (<40.5 ha smallholder)
- **Facilities:** triangle pattern **~136–148 palms/ha (~55/acre)**; harvesting paths + collection ramps, all-weather FFB roads (mill within ~24 h to limit FFA), drains/terraces on slopes, fertiliser store, **contracted mill within haulage** (smallholders sell FFB, don't mill). Legal smallholder <40.46 ha (100 acres).
- **Equipment:** young palms — **chisel (dodos)** or MPOB motorised **"CKAT"**; tall — **sickle (egrek) on pole** or MPOB motorised **"Cantas"**; wheelbarrows/mini-grabber for FFB evacuation, knapsack sprayers, **loose-fruit collection tools**.
- **Inputs:** **DxP planting material** (never recycled dura); scheduled **inorganic fertiliser** (N/P/K/Mg/B — biggest cost + main yield lever, smallholder gap = under-fertilising); **EFB + POME recycled** as mulch/fertiliser; legume cover crops (Mucuna/Pueraria); circle-weeding herbicide.
- **Varieties:** **DxP (Dura×Pisifera→Tenera)** — Deli dura × AVROS pisifera standard; MPOB clones (CPS3) higher yield.
- **Scale/yields:** good DxP **~24 t FFB/ha/yr** (best ~28–29; national smallholder ~17–19 due to agronomy gap); bears yr 3, peaks yr 7–15, ~25-yr life.
- **Workflow:** defining rhythm = **10–12 day harvest round** per block (cut ripe FFB judged by loose fruit, lower, collect loose fruit, haul same-day to mill); frond pruning (don't over-prune), circle-weed, fertiliser 2–4×/yr, pest census; mid-year + late-year yield peaks.
- **Labour:** **skilled harvesters** (judge ripeness, handle pole-sickle — physical/safety-critical) + fertiliser/spray/prune; small permanent crew + harvest labour; CKAT/Cantas/mini-transporters cut headcount.
- **Economics:** FFB tracks CPO — MPOB projects **CPO ~RM 4,000–4,300/t through 2026** → FFB ~RM 800–1,000+/t; levers = fertiliser discipline + tight harvesting + OER; fertiliser is dominant cost.
- **Regs:** **MPOB licence mandatory** (plant/sell/transport FFB); **MSPO compulsory** (6 mo from licence); replanting (TSPKS) + input (IPPKS) schemes.
- **Risks:** **Ganoderma basal stem rot** (No.1, no cure — sanitation + resistant material), rhino beetle (young palms), bagworm/nettle caterpillar, rats (barn-owl biocontrol), crown/spear rot; drought/El Niño cuts bunches 1–2 yr later; flooding; price volatility.
- **Circular:** **fronds → in-field mulch**; **EFB → mulch/compost/biochar feedstock**; **POME → biogas** (on-site power for off-grid microgrid) + digestate fertiliser; kernel shell/fibre → biomass/biochar; legume cover cuts fertiliser.
- Sources: [MPOB planting density](https://palmoilis.mpob.gov.my/publications/OPIEJ/opiejv3n2-32.pdf) · [MPOB Cantas/CKAT](http://tot.mpob.gov.my/tt-no-409-motorized-chisel-ckattm-for-short-palm-harvesting/) · [MSPO FAQ](https://mspo.org.my/faqs/) · [EFB+POME biogas](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0960148121010788)

# BEEKEEPING

Two parallel enterprises sharing little equipment. **Kelulut (stingless)** = commercial + agritourism
anchor (low-volume, high-value, sting-free, ideal for tropical MY). **Apis** = more honey + orchard
pollination but needs protective handling.

## Kelulut (stingless bees — Meliponini)
- **Species:** **_Heterotrigona itama_** (hardiest, industry default) + **_Geniotrigona thoracica_** (prized honey, strong pollinator).
- **Facilities/equipment:** log ("gelodok") or stacked **wooden box hives (OATH-style)** for split/harvest; hives on shaded stands with **ant moats/grease bands**; foraging range only ~500 m so site inside the forage. **No suit/veil/smoker needed** — honey in propolis pots, harvested by **food-grade suction pump**; jars, fine strainer, refractometer (honey ~25–35% moisture, ferments easily), cool storage.
- **Inputs:** starter log ~RM 150–250 (~RM 1,500 for a 3-colony micro-farm); self-propagate by **splitting**; plant continuous-bloom forage (*Antigonon*, *Turnera* + orchard/estate crops); syrup only in monsoon dearth.
- **Yields/economics:** only **~4–6 kg/colony/yr**; serious farm 50–1,000+ colonies (~RM 40–50k mid-size → RM 5–30k/mo); **~40-day harvest cycle**. Premium: farmgate ~RM 60/kg, retail **RM 120–150/kg** (multiples of Apis); co-products propolis/pollen/cerumen.
- **Regs:** **MS 2683:2017** kelulut honey standard; **MyGAP-AM** (Meliponini) + **MeSTI** + Halal for processing/retail.
- **Risks:** small hive beetles (~21%), ants (~20%), spiders, termites; **black soldier fly (*Hermetia illucens*)** catastrophic (wiped thousands of colonies in a week in 2016 — colonises weak hives); phorid flies, geckos, hornets, monsoon forage scarcity, fermentation, **pesticide drift**. Keep colonies strong.

## Apis (honey bees)
- **Species:** native **_Apis cerana_** (heat/mite-tolerant, box-manageable — the practical choice); _A. mellifera_ higher yield but imported/heat-prone; _A. dorsata_ (tualang) is wild-harvested, not hive-kept.
- **Equipment/yields:** Langstroth/Newton box hives + **smoker, veil/suit, hive tool, centrifugal extractor**; _cerana_ single-digit to ~20 kg/yr, mellifera 25–60 kg; retail ~RM 30–60/kg. **Main value = orchard/crop pollination.**

**Cross-cutting:** daily entrance/ant check; weekly–monthly inspections; **split** to expand + harvest kelulut ~40-day / Apis at nectar flows. Kelulut = low-risk marquee **agritourism station** (hive tours, live suction-harvest, tastings selling honey at RM 120–150/kg direct). Pollination lifts fruit set across orchard + hydro/aqua fruiting crops.
- Sources: [Kelulut farming, Vulcan Post](https://vulcanpost.com/742509/kelulut-stingless-bee-honey-farm-malaysia/) · [Kelulut in Sabah](https://www.mysabah.com/wordpress/stingless-bee-kelulut-farming-sabah/) · [Reinventing Honey Industry, PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6422541/) · [MS 2683](https://foodcomplianceinternational.com/industry-insight/news/5065-new-standard-on-kelulut-stingless-bee-honey) · [BSF infestation of hives](https://innspub.net/jbes/mass-infestation-black-soldier-fly-hermetia-illucens-diptera-stratiomyidae-colonies-indo-malayan-stingless-bees-geniotrigona-thoracica-heterotrigona-itama/)

# PROCESSING & CIRCULAR

## Food Processing & Value-Add
- **Facilities:** **dedicated processing room** (not the restaurant kitchen) with MeSTI/Halal hygiene zones — dirty/receiving → clean/processing → packing/storage, one-way flow; impervious surfaces, coved skirting, drains, handwash, fly screens; chiller/freezer + low-humidity dry store; separate pasteurising bay for dairy.
- **Equipment:** **pressure canner/retort** (mandatory for low-acid, 116 °C to kill *C. botulinum*); **boiling-water-bath** only for high-acid (pH ≤4.6); steam-jacketed kettle, pH meter + refractometer, pasteuriser, bottle/induction/vacuum sealers; **freeze-dryer** (~USD 14k, ~25-yr shelf life — best for fruit/herbs, not high-sugar); dehydrator/solar dryer; labeller/date coder.
- **Inputs:** glut + "seconds" produce (loss-recovery), packaging (single-use lids), sugar/salt/vinegar/citric acid/pectin. Freeze-dryer + pressure canner are **heavy sustained off-grid loads — schedule for midday solar** (or biogas heat).
- **Scale:** jam/sauce 20–50 jars/batch; canner 7–16 jars/cycle; freeze-dryer ~3–4 kg dry/24 h.
- **Workflow:** receive/wash/sort → cook-and-fill batches → sterilise → label/date/quarantine until seal check; **always a validated recipe, no acid/sugar substitutions**; peaks in harvest gluts, build shelf-stable inventory for lean months.
- **Labour:** ≥1 **food-safety-trained** operator (MeSTI/GHP, HACCP if exporting); safe canning/acidification, thermal processing, seal verification, allergen/label compliance, batch records.
- **Economics:** several-fold value uplift; converts perishable price-volatile produce to stable inventory; costs = packaging + energy + labour.
- **Regs:** **MeSTI** baseline (MOH) → GMP → **HACCP** (export); **JAKIM Halal**; US export = **FDA FSMA** (filed scheduled process for LACF/acidified).
- **Risks:** **botulism** (low-acid must be pressure-canned or acidified <pH 4.6), under-processing/seal failure, tropical-humidity mould/mycotoxins on dried goods, allergen mislabel, cold-chain breaks.
- **Circular:** trim/peel/spoiled batches + whey/brine → **BSF + biogas** feedstock; process heat/hot water from **biogas**; glass reuse.
- Sources: [pressure canning, Virginia Tech](https://www.pubs.ext.vt.edu/348/348-585/348-585.html) · [CDC home-canned botulism](https://www.cdc.gov/botulism/prevention/home-canned-foods.html) · [freeze-drying value-add](https://cfgrower.com/freeze-drying-another-way-to-add-value/) · [MeSTI/HACCP/GMP](https://www.certlabs.co/your-guide-to-food-safety-certifications-haccp-gmp-and-mesti/) · [JAKIM halal](https://www.ajobthing.com/resources/blog/jakim-halal-certification-requirements-and-application-process)

## Recycling / Resource Recovery (the circular hub)
Four interlocking subsystems: **biogas · BSF protein · biochar · compost/vermicompost + greywater.**
- **Facilities:** **biogas** — fixed-dome concrete (~50 m³, ~50-day HRT, mesophilic ~35–37 °C easy in MY) or HDPE tubular, gas in dome/bladder; **BSF** — screened rearing house + mating "love cage" + self-harvest larvae bins (~27 °C/70% RH, moisture the key challenge); **biochar** — drum-retort or **flame-curtain (Kon-Tiki)** kiln (300–500 °C slow pyrolysis of palm/wood); **compost** windrows + shaded vermicompost beds; **greywater** — multistage **constructed wetland** (>90% organic removal) → subsurface drip to orchard.
- **Equipment:** digester + gas holder + **H₂S scrubber (iron sponge)** + moisture trap + flame arrestor + biogas stove/genset; BSF cages/bins/sieve/dryer(+oil press); kiln + chipper + quench; windrow turner + thermometer/moisture meter + grease trap.
- **Inputs:** digester — manure + **POME** + food waste + crop residue + whey/brine (co-digest manure+food waste best; kitchen waste energy-dense); BSF — same organic stream (larvae eat ~50% BW/day, cut waste 50–80%); biochar — EFB/fronds/trunk/shell + wood/orchard prunings + coconut husk; compost — browns+greens+**BSF frass**, moistened with greywater.
- **Scale:** POME ~28 m³ biogas/t; ~5 m³ digester ≈ 120 kg/day feedstock @40-day HRT; biogas ~60–70% CH₄. BSF harvest **14–18 d**, ~25–30% of input weight, dried larvae **40–50% protein/~35% fat** (≈ fishmeal). Compost tonnes/mo; wetland sized to daily greywater m³.
- **Workflow:** digester fed daily (feed:water ~1:2), gas drawn continuously, digestate removed, monitor pressure/pH; BSF rolling 14–18 d cycle; biochar batch burns → quench → charge with compost/digestate; compost turned on schedule; wetland flow monitored.
- **Labour:** biogas technician (AD + gas safety), BSF operator (colony/moisture/harvest), kiln operator (fire safety), compost/wetland caretaker — cross-trained; life-support (colony, digester) survives outages.
- **Economics:** biogas offsets LPG/diesel/grid (cooking + processing + small genset) + free digestate; **BSF = protein (feed substitute) + frass (premium fertiliser)** — MY suppliers (BioLoop, Biovae) sell BSF meal + frass; **biochar** ~USD 116–197/t, net-negative emissions (−436 to −2,085 kg CO₂e/t) → soil value + carbon-credit potential.
- **Regs:** **DOE** effluent (POME/digestate), open-burning restrictions favour kilns; **DVS** feed regs if selling BSF meal (feedstock provenance matters); biogas safety (H₂S removal, flame arrestors, ventilation).
- **Risks:** **biogas gas safety** (explosive + toxic H₂S + asphyxiant — scrub/ventilate/leak-check/no ignition), BSF moisture/temperature/pathogen control, biochar fire/PAH if too hot/dirty, compost/wetland pathogens+odour if under-processed.
- **The loops:** `manure+POME+food+processing waste → biogas → cooking/heat/electricity` · `digestate → liquid fertiliser (hydro/aqua make-up, orchard, veg)` · `organic waste → BSF larvae → poultry/fish/zoo feed; frass → soil` · `palm+wood → biochar → carbon-rich soil` · `fish/aquaponics water → irrigation; greywater → wetland → drip fruit trees`. **Waste from every vertical becomes an input to another.**
- Sources: [tropical dome digester / POME](https://www.snv.org/assets/downloads/f/191310/9501da0c59/palm_oil_waste_biogas.pdf) · [UPM tropical BSF](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s43621-025-02250-1) · [oil-palm biochar review](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/353068784_A_review_on_sustainability_and_quality_of_biochar_production_from_oil_palm_biomass_in_Malaysia_using_thermal_conversion_technology) · [constructed wetland tropical](https://mdpi.com/2313-4321/6/4/63/htm) · [BioLoop MY BSF](https://www.bioloop.com.my/) · [biochar economics/GWP](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0048969724004017)

# HOSPITALITY & AGRITOURISM

## Lodging (eco-hotel ~60 rooms + glamping + cabins)
- **Facilities:** three zoned tiers; **domes** (4–6 m geodesic PVC/PC on decks, screened, **AC non-negotiable**, ~RM 40–120k all-in each); **cabins** (timber A-frames on stilts — flood/termite, verandah, fans+split AC, rainwater toilets); central/outsourced laundry, 3× linen par, generator/solar backup. **60 rooms + domes on AC = largest discretionary electrical draw** → shed guest-room AC with warning, never cut pumps to occupied units.
- **Systems:** **Beds24** PMS + channel manager (one inventory across 50+ OTAs, no overbooking) + direct booking engine (dodge 15–18% OTA commission), **digital door locks/keypads** (scattered site), guest WhatsApp; **Tourism Tax RM 10/room/night (foreign)** + **8% SST** above RM 500k turnover configured in PMS.
- **Ops/labour:** check-in 3pm/out 12pm, buggy transfers; housekeeping ~1 attendant/10–14 rooms (domes/cabins ~1.3 room-equiv); realistic ~2–3 front desk, 5–7 housekeeping, 3–4 maintenance/grounds, 1 duty manager (~1.0–1.5 staff/room, lean end + outsource laundry/landscaping).
- **Economics:** domes RM 250–700 (premium 600–1,200), cabins RM 300–600, hotel rooms RM 200–400; plan **rural occupancy 45–60%** (weekend/school-holiday skew); manage **RevPAR** not headline ADR.
- **Regs:** **MOTAC registration** (Tourism Industry Act 1992); **local council (PBT) licence first**, then MOTAC; new **STRA guidelines** (state caps, e.g. Selangor 180 nights/yr from 2026); **BOMBA** fire cert + CCC + public liability.
- **Risks:** monsoon seasonality + tent/deck damage, mould/termite/flood on timber/domes, OTA-review clusters, outage complaints (mitigated by FarmOS resilience banner), STRA tightening.
- **Integration:** domes/cabins monetise the working farm — sunrise farm walks, egg collecting, harvest breakfast, stargazing; solar+rainwater story is the eco-marketing hook.

## Farm-to-Table Restaurant
- **Facilities:** rain-protected open dining pavilion + show/harvest kitchen + back kitchen; ~120–180 seats + event overflow (150–300 guests); **cold chain is the spine** (walk-in chiller/freezer, blast-cool, raw/cooked separation, short farm-to-kitchen transfer) — **cold storage = life-support/never-shed**; grease trap, water treatment, dry store, wash line.
- **Systems:** **Loyverse** (or F&B POS) tied to PMS (room charging) + feeding FarmOS covers/revenue; KDS, recipe/portion costing, **stock integration back to farm production** ("what's ripe" drives menu); **HACCP temp logging** into FarmOS, pest control, fire suppression.
- **Ops/labour:** **seasonal harvest-led menu** (flex to same-day yield); breakfast (in-house)/lunch (day-trippers/tours)/dinner (destination); pre-order for tour groups; head chef + sous + 3–5 cooks + 2 stewards + restaurant manager + 6–10 servers; dedicated purchaser liaises daily with farm.
