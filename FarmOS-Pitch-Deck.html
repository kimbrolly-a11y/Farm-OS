The problem

One person. Ten businesses.
A hundred off-grid acres.

A single founder is opening lodging, a restaurant, hydroponics, aquaponics, an orchard, poultry, a petting zoo, palm oil, recycling and bees — on one 100-acre site in Malaysia, with its own solar and water and no reliable grid.

Each vertical is a full business with its own sensors, staff and failure modes. Run by hand, this needs a team of ten. One person can't hold it in their head — and if the internet drops, the tools stop.

FarmOS
2
The insight
Not ten businesses.
One platform, ten modules.

Treat the farm as a single operating system with plug-in modules on one shared life-support layer. That's what makes it runnable by one person — and replicable to the next farm from a config file.

3
The solution

FarmOS: a digital twin + an autonomous Claude agent

The agent is the operations team. It reads the whole farm, decides across every vertical, acts through real devices, and logs every decision — so one founder runs ten departments.

Autonomous

A Claude supervisor agent perceives, decides and acts across ten domains with no human in the loop.

Sovereign

Runs on-site on solar and local compute. Keeps operating through a grid + internet blackout.

Replicable

A new farm is a new config file, not a new build. One system, many sites.

Sovereignty

When the world goes dark,
the company keeps running.

Solar and rainwater for power and water. Home Assistant and the agent's fallback logic run on a Raspberry Pi on-site. In a Malaysian monsoon blackout, FarmOS sheds non-critical loads, keeps life-support alive, and queues the outside world for when it returns.
